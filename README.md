## Research Project: LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

### Why I chose this topic
Out of the 8 available topics, I chose LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy 
because it aligns closely with my own ongoing learning — I'm actively 
building my professional presence on LinkedIn while job hunting, so 
studying how top practitioners approach content strategy gave me both a 
research task and a personal skill upgrade.

### What I collected
- **10 verified LinkedIn practitioners** who actively practice organic 
content strategy for B2B SaaS (not just theorists or agencies)
- **30 LinkedIn posts** (3 per expert), organized by author in 
`/research/linkedin-posts/`
- **3 YouTube video transcripts** from experts who also publish video 
content, organized in `/research/youtube-transcripts/`:
  - Lara Acosta — LinkedIn algorithm research breakdown
  - Amanda Natividad — marketing stack consolidation trends
  - Sangram Vajre — GTM strategy framework (8 questions)
- **1 podcast appearance summary** in `/research/other/`:
  - Katelyn Bourgoin on Creator Science (guest appearance — her own 
  podcast, Customer Show, appears inactive since ~2021 based on 
  verification research)
- Full source list with links, verification dates, and selection rationale 
in `/research/sources.md`

### How I selected these experts
Each expert was personally verified on LinkedIn before being added to the 
list, based on:
- Active, consistent posting within the last 1–2 months
- Being a genuine practitioner — someone who has built real, verifiable 
results (not just someone who writes about theory)
- Content specific and relevant to B2B SaaS, not generic personal branding 
advice
- Real engagement (comments and discussion, not just passive likes)

During verification, I also corrected an initial assumption — I had 
originally noted Katelyn Bourgoin as actively running her own podcast, but 
further research showed her own show has been inactive since around 2021. 
Her recent podcast presence is as a guest on other shows, which I updated 
in `sources.md` accordingly.

### Observations across the collected content

**From LinkedIn posts:**
- Personal narrative is the dominant format. Most high-performing posts 
use a before/after or personal-story structure rather than direct advice 
or listicles.
- Data-driven posts build authority — practitioners like Kyle Poyar use 
original research/reports to position themselves as trusted sources.
- Almost every post ends with a soft CTA (free guide, newsletter, 
waitlist) rather than a hard sales pitch.
- Vulnerability and behind-the-scenes content performs well, even from 
highly successful founders (e.g., admitting mistakes, sharing failures 
alongside wins).

**From YouTube transcripts:**
- Lara Acosta's breakdown of LinkedIn's algorithm research directly 
supports the "specific beats generic" pattern seen across the LinkedIn 
posts — profiles and content that are narrowly positioned get better 
algorithmic distribution than broad, vague ones.
- Amanda Natividad's marketing stack analysis, while not LinkedIn-specific, 
reinforces a broader theme across the research: successful B2B 
practitioners are willing to publicly reverse or question popular 
assumptions rather than repeat conventional wisdom.
- Sangram Vajre's 8-question GTM framework shows how the same practitioners 
teaching content strategy are also operators applying structured thinking 
to their own businesses — content advice is backed by operating 
experience, not just theory.

**From the podcast appearance:**
- Katelyn Bourgoin's "chocolate covered almond" framework for content 
(tempting hook/ending wrapped around substantive value) offers a concrete 
mental model that ties together why the LinkedIn posts collected in this 
research consistently pair strong hooks with real lessons.
- Her transparent post-mortem of a recent cohort launch (pricing mistakes, 
positioning gaps) is a useful reminder that even experienced practitioners 
iterate publicly and share failures, which matches the "vulnerability 
performs well" pattern observed in the LinkedIn posts.
